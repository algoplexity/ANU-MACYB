### I. Primary Node: ANU Centre for Energy Systems (ACES)

ACES is the central hub for modeling complex energy manifolds, incorporating the Battery Storage and Grid Integration Program (BSGIP) and the 100 Percent Renewable Energy Group (RE100)].

* **Complexity Mapping (Energy System Modeling):** Theme 2 of BSGIP focuses on modeling, forecasting, and prediction of power systems with high penetration of distributed energy resources (DER)]. From an AIT perspective, this is a search for the minimal program $p$ that describes grid state transitions.
* **Algorithmic Control and Coordination:** Theme 3 focuses on the orchestration of battery systems and electric vehicles]. This aligns with Titan’s mandate to treat systems as deterministic control processes. A placement here allows for testing AIT-based control rules against real-world grid data].
* **Opportunities for Master's/PhD Pathways:** ACES frequently seeks expressions of interest (EOI) for research initiatives such as "neighbourhood battery" decarbonization and rooftop solar integration]. These projects represent discrete computational branches where a student node can contribute to reducing the description length of urban energy flows].
